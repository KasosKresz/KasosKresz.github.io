About Me

Krisztian Kasos, PhD is a psychologist and researcher specializing in stress and anxiety regulation, emotional processes, and behavioural science.

He holds a PhD in Psychology and has worked as a researcher and assistant professor, studying emotional regulation, anxiety, and psychophysiological responses to stress.

His work includes stress and anxiety management, guided meditation, relaxation techniques, mindfulness-based approaches, biofeedback, and cognitive-behavioural methods.

Through workshops and guided practices, he aims to help people better understand stress, develop practical coping tools, and build healthier ways of responding to challenging situations.

Workshops

These workshops are designed for people who would like to better understand how stress and anxiety affect the mind and body, and how to respond to them in healthier, more balanced ways.

Participants are introduced to practical tools such as breathing exercises, relaxation techniques, grounding methods, guided meditation, and cognitive-behavioural strategies that can support emotional wellbeing and resilience.
